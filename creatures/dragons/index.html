Dragons

What are they?

The word "Dragon" come from the Latin word draconem which means “huge serpent, dragon,” which in turn is from the Greek word drakon that means “serpent, giant seafish.” Dragon now days is usually used in the form of a mythical monster that looks like a giant reptile with wings and breathes fire. With most descriptions of these creatures being the same there are a few stand out exceptions to the norm. One example is in the Wheel of Time book series by Robert Jordan. The dragon in those books is a title given to a male champion of the Light against the Dark One. With how many different stories and movies contain dragons, it becomes difficult to know what a true dragon is and what is not.

Different types

True Dragons

True Dragons are dragons that have four legs and two wings that are seperate to the legs. True Dragons are the types of dragons found in D&D as well as most of the dragons in the How To Train Your Dragon movies. They are normally able to fly and sometimes have their own form of magic. The dragons in D&D that are True Dragons come in different colors that denote what their magical abilities are. There are Red, Green, Black, White, and Blue Dragons that control fire, poison, acid, ice, and lightning respectively. There are also the metallic dragons that are also True Dragons. They are Brass, Bronze, Copper, Gold, Silver. They control fire, lightning, acid, fire, and ice respectively.

Wyverns

Wyverns are dragons that have two legs and two wings that also act as their front arms. Examples would include the Hungarian Horntail from Harry Potter and The Goblet of Fire, Smaug from The Hobbit, and the dragons in the TV show Game of Thrones. While they may be considered dragons, it is more accurate to call them Wyverns because it is more accurate.

Other Examples

Other types of dragons would include Drakes, Amphitheres, Lung Dragons, Lindwurms, and Wyrms. Drakes are four legged dragons without any wings. Amphitheres are the opposite with two wings and no legs. Lung Dragons are long bodied four legged dragons also known as oriental dragons. Lindwurms are long bodied dragons that only have two front legs. Wyrms are legless long bodied dragons.
